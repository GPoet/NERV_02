## 1. AGENCY BRAND IDENTITY RECOMMENDATIONS

### The Core Question: Should the consultancy BE "NERV"?

**Recommendation: No. NERV should NOT be the consultancy name.**

Here is the reasoning:

NERV is an Evangelion reference. It works beautifully as a product name — dark, technical, insider-coded. But as a consultancy brand that invoices companies and signs contracts, it carries three risks:

1. **IP risk.** NERV is directly from Neon Genesis Evangelion. Gainax/Khara hold rights. For a portfolio project this is fine. For a revenue-generating entity, it creates trademark exposure.
2. **Audience ceiling.** Enterprise buyers and startup founders who are not anime fans will miss the reference entirely and see just a four-letter word. The brand needs to work for people who do not know what an AT Field is.
3. **Brand architecture constraint.** NERV already means something specific — your autonomous agent system, the dashboard, the command interface. Forcing it to also mean "the consultancy that builds things for clients" dilutes both meanings.

**The play:** Keep NERV as the flagship product/platform identity. Build a parent brand that can house NERV, Paperclip deployments, client work, and future offerings.

---

### Name Options for the Consultancy Entity

#### Option 1: SIGNAL (Recommended)

**Rationale:** Your existing brand language already uses signal aesthetics — the NERV logo system, the monospace type, the orange-on-dark palette. "Signal" is technical without being obscure. It implies clarity amid noise, intelligence extraction, pattern recognition. It works in every context: "Signal built our agent system" / "Signal's NERV platform" / "We engaged Signal for the implementation."

**Domain potential:** signal.agency, signalops.dev, getsignal.ai
**Trademark:** Clean in the AI consultancy space (distinct from the messaging app)

#### Option 2: MAGI

**Rationale:** Already in your codebase (MAGI-SYS in the terminal). Three supercomputers that reach consensus — a perfect metaphor for multi-agent orchestration. Deeply technical, anime-coded for insiders, but reads as "wise counsel" to everyone else (the biblical Magi).

**Domain potential:** magi.systems, magiops.ai
**Risk:** Still anime-adjacent, though less legally exposed than NERV

#### Option 3: Tensor Works

**Rationale:** Sounds like an engineering firm. "Tensor" signals deep ML/AI competence. "Works" implies a workshop — craft, precision, building things. Clean, professional, scalable.

**Domain potential:** tensor.works, tensorworks.ai
**Positioning:** More enterprise-friendly, less personality

#### Option 4: Null Agency

**Rationale:** Programmer humor that lands. "Null" means empty, undefined — the starting state before your agents fill it. Provocative, memorable, very in-brand with the cyberpunk aesthetic. Works as a creative agency name.

**Domain potential:** null.agency, nullagency.dev
**Risk:** Some clients may not get it; slightly too clever for enterprise

#### Option 5: Aeon Labs

**Rationale:** You already use "Aeon" as the autonomous agent identity. "Labs" signals R&D capability. This is the most directly connected to your existing work.

**Domain potential:** aeonlabs.ai, aeon-labs.dev
**Risk:** "Aeon" is used by many companies. Less distinctive. Also merges your personal agent identity with the commercial entity.

## 2. BRAND VOICE & POSITIONING

### Positioning Statement

**For** technical founders and engineering leaders **who** need autonomous AI agent systems but lack the in-house expertise to build them, **[SIGNAL]** is a specialist AI agent consultancy **that** designs, builds, and deploys production-grade multi-agent systems. **Unlike** generic AI consultancies that deliver slide decks, **we** ship working infrastructure — because we built our own first.

### Tagline Options

1. **"We built our agents. Now we'll build yours."**
   Direct. Proves capability through autobiography. Works in every context.

2. **"Autonomous systems for non-trivial problems."**
   Filters for serious buyers. Signals that you do not do chatbot wrappers.

3. **"The signal in the noise."**
   Works if SIGNAL is the brand name. Positions as the clarity provider in a hype-saturated market.

4. **"441 agents. Zero hallucinations about what works."**
   Specific, provocative, uses the Paperclip number as social proof.

5. **"From terminal to production."**
   Technical, concise. Implies the full journey — not just prototypes.

6. **"Infrastructure-grade AI. Boutique-grade attention."**
   Positions the tension between scale and care. Good for enterprise pitches.

7. **"Your AI agents should work as hard as ours do."**
   Slightly aggressive. Implies your system is battle-tested, theirs is not.

### Elevator Pitches

**For technical founders (30 seconds):**
"I build autonomous AI agent systems — not chatbots, not copilots, actual infrastructure that runs your operations. I've deployed a 441-agent orchestration platform, built an autonomous agent that runs 41 skills on GitHub Actions, and shipped a real-time intelligence dashboard. Now I help companies build the same thing, customized for their domain. First engagement starts with a 2-week architecture sprint."

**For investors/advisors (30 seconds):**
"Every company will need AI agents, but most can't build them. I'm a specialist consultancy focused entirely on multi-agent system design and deployment. I've already built and open-sourced the infrastructure. My first client came through reputation alone. I'm packaging that expertise as a productized service — architecture sprints, implementation, and ongoing operations."

**For other developers (15 seconds):**
"I run a consultancy that designs and deploys multi-agent AI systems. I built NERV — an autonomous agent on GitHub Actions with 41 skills — and run Paperclip for 441-agent orchestration. If you need production agent infrastructure, that's what I do."

### Tone of Voice Guidelines

| Dimension | The Voice | NOT This |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Technical depth** | Specific. Cite numbers, tools, architectures. "441 agents on claude-sonnet-4-6 at $0.50/heartbeat." | Vague. "We leverage cutting-edge AI to transform your business." |
| **Confidence** | Earned. "We built this. Here's the repo." | Arrogant. "We're the best AI agency." |
| **Aesthetic** | Dark mode. Monospace. Controlled tension. | Bright, bubbly startup energy. |
| **Complexity** | Respect the reader's intelligence. Do not simplify for simplification's sake. | Dumbed-down explanations. "AI is like a really smart helper." |
| **Humor** | Dry. Terminal-coded. `// TODO: acquire more clients` | Forced. Emoji-heavy. LinkedIn-bro energy. |
| **Urgency** | Calm competence. The system runs itself. | Hype. "AI is moving SO FAST you need us NOW." |

**Writing rules:**
- Lead with specifics, not adjectives
- Use monospace for technical terms in marketing copy
- Short paragraphs. Whitespace is a design element.
- Never use: "leverage," "synergy," "disrupt," "game-changing," "at scale" (unless literally about infrastructure scale)
- Always attribute capabilities to real systems: NERV, Paperclip, OpenClaw — not abstract claims

## 4. CREDIBILITY BUILDING

### Turning Client #1 into a Case Study

This is the single highest-leverage marketing asset you can create.

**Step 1: Ask permission now.**
Frame it as: "I'd like to write up our work together as a reference project. I can anonymize details if needed. Would you be comfortable with that?" Most clients say yes because it also makes them look innovative.

**Step 2: Document while the engagement is fresh.**
Write the case study using the format above. Include:
- Before/after architecture diagrams
- Specific numbers (agents deployed, time saved, capabilities enabled)
- A direct quote from the client (even 1 sentence)

**Step 3: Create three versions.**
- Full case study (PDF + blog post)
- 1-paragraph summary (for the landing page)
- Twitter thread version (for launch week)

**Step 4: Gate the full version if useful.**
Consider requiring an email to access the full PDF. Builds your lead list from day one.

### Open Source Positioning: Paperclip as Credibility Signal

Paperclip is your most powerful credibility asset. Here is how to position it:

1. **Star count matters.** Add a compelling README with demo GIF, clear value prop, and quick-start guide. Submit to Hacker News, relevant subreddits, and AI newsletters.

2. **Position it as "the infrastructure we use for client deployments."** This transforms open source from "hobby project" to "battle-tested production tool."

3. **Write the Paperclip launch blog post.** Title: "We Open-Sourced Our 441-Agent Orchestration Platform." Walk through the architecture, design decisions, and what you learned running it in production.

4. **Create a "Powered by Paperclip" badge.** When you deploy client systems, include this. Builds the brand through usage.

5. **Contributor engagement.** Respond to issues within 24 hours. Accept thoughtful PRs. This signals an active, maintained project.

## Summary

**The strategic move:** Create a parent consultancy brand (SIGNAL recommended) that houses NERV as the flagship product and Paperclip as the open-source credibility engine. This gives you room to grow beyond a single product identity while keeping the technical edge that differentiates you.

**The creative move:** Your existing NERV aesthetic IS your brand. Do not soften it. The dark mode terminal look, the amber-on-dark palette, the monospace typography — this is what makes you memorable in a sea of gradient-washed AI startup sites. Lean into it harder.

**The GTM move:** Week 1 is about presence. Week 2 is about proof. Week 3 is about amplification. Week 4 is about systems. By end of month 1, you should have: a live landing page, 3 blog posts, 1 case study, 1 demo video, an active social presence, and 5+ pipeline conversations.

**The single most important thing to do first:** Ask Client #1 for case study permission. Every other asset is strengthened by having one real engagement to reference.
